The problem

You don't know what you don't know.

There is a waterfall in Manhattan. A hidden mews two blocks from your office. A street where, twice a year, the sunset lines up perfectly with the grid. Most of the city is invisible to the people who live in it, not because it's hidden, but because no one ever points it out.

You're walking anyway. Why not take the route full of experiences?

How a walk works

Tell Ramble what kind of walk you want.

Take me on a loop

Say how much time you have. Ramble builds a loop through the most beautiful streets nearby and brings you back where you started.

Going somewhere?

Headed to dinner, the park, a friend's place? Ramble finds the prettiest way there, not just the fastest.

Find me a pretty spot

No plan, no destination. Ramble points you to a bench by the water, a pocket park, a view worth sitting with.

Under every route is a scored map of all 337,000 street segments in New York, built with MIT backed research on what makes a street worth walking down.

Earlier this month

One evening this July, hundreds of strangers stopped mid block to watch the sun set straight down the avenue. The city put on a show. Most people found out after it was over.

There are countless moments like these. Don't miss them.

Why walk this way

Experiencing the world is not a passive act.

Notice more.

The prewar block, the ginkgo in bloom, the light hitting the water. Attention is a skill, and walking is where you practice it.

Feel how alive the city can be.

Every neighborhood has a rhythm. Ramble routes you through it instead of around it.

Enter your flow state.

No pace. No calories. No feed. Just you, moving through a beautiful place with nowhere else to be.

Fits into your life

Your calendar

Sync your calendar and Ramble will craft walks that fit your open windows. Forty free minutes becomes a loop through the neighborhood.
